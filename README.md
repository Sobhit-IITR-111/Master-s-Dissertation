# Master's Dissertation | Rock Physics & Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS)
Topic: "Quantifying Texturally Controlled Physical and Seismic Rock Properties in Pre- and Post-CO₂ Sequestered Basalts"

# Research Gap:
Despite the growing interest in Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS) within basaltic formations, several critical scientific gaps remain, particularly for the Deccan Trap Basalts of India.

1) Most previous studies have focused on CO₂ mineralization and geochemical reactions, while the corresponding evolution of seismic properties during sequestration has received limited attention.
2) Existing research generally investigates either pore-scale characterization (SEM/micro-CT) or ultrasonic rock physics, but very few studies establish a direct quantitative relationship between microstructural evolution and seismic responses.
3) The influence of pore aspect ratio, heterogeneity, and seismic anisotropy on simultaneous changes in P-wave velocity (Vp), S-wave velocity (Vs), and attenuation (Qp and Qs) before and after CO₂ exposure has not been systematically investigated.
4) Previous laboratory studies predominantly emphasize velocity measurements, whereas dual-wave attenuation (Qp and Qs), a highly sensitive indicator of pore-fluid interaction and rock-frame modification—remains largely unexplored for basaltic reservoirs.
5) For Indian Deccan Basalts, an integrated rock physics dataset combining micro-CT, ultrasonic measurements, attenuation analysis, and pore geometry evolution was unavailable, limiting the development of seismic monitoring strategies for future CCS projects.

# Problem Statement:
India's Deccan Trap Basalts possess enormous potential for permanent geological storage of CO₂ through mineral carbonation. However, there is currently no quantitative rock physics framework capable of relating pore-scale modifications induced by CO₂ sequestration to measurable seismic responses.
Without understanding how pore geometry, heterogeneity, and anisotropy evolve during CO₂-rock interaction and influence velocity and attenuation, field-scale monitoring techniques such as time-lapse (4D) seismic surveys cannot be interpreted reliably.

Therefore, a comprehensive framework is required to:
i) quantify pore-scale structural evolution,
ii) measure corresponding changes in elastic and attenuation properties,
iii) establish the relationship between microstructure and seismic response, and
iv) identify reliable seismic indicators for monitoring different stages of CO₂ sequestration in basalt reservoirs.

# Methodology & Inferences:
1) Investigated the impact of CO₂ sequestration on the elastic, attenuation, and microstructural properties of Deccan basalt samples using ultrasonic laboratory measurements and digital rock analysis.
2) Developed Python-based workflows for automated P-, Sv-, and Sh-wave processing, spectral-ratio attenuation analysis (Qp and Qs estimation), interactive arrival picking, and real-time frequency-domain characterization.
3) Quantified pore-scale textural evolution through pore aspect ratio analysis from digital rock volumes and linked microstructural changes to variations in seismic velocities, attenuation coefficients, and quality factors.
4) Established relationships between attenuation attributes (Qp, Qs, Qs/Qp), elastic parameters (Vp, Vs, Vp/Vs, Poisson's ratio), seismic anisotropy, and pore geometry to characterize fluid-rock interactions induced by CO₂ mineralization.
5) Demonstrated the potential of the **Qs/Qp–Vp/Vs state-space framework** as a fluid discriminator for tracking CO₂ saturation and pore-fluid evolution in basaltic reservoirs.
6) Contributed toward understanding how pore-scale alterations caused by CO₂ sequestration may influence larger-scale seismic observables, supporting future CCS monitoring strategies in basalt formations by developing a laboratory-scale rock physics framework for interpreting time-lapse seismic responses associated with different stages of CO2 sequestration.

# Summary: 
I worked on investigating the effectiveness and feasibility of CO2 sequestration in basalts by quantifying the physical the seismic rock properties in Pre and Post CO2 state that will help us to constitute the rock physics framework which builds the gap between micro scale textural properties evolution with the macro-scale time lapse-seismic monitoring of CO2.
